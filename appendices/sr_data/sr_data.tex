\section{Electronic Properties}

Strontium has two principal transitions from the $\nSLJ{5s^2}{1}{S}{0}$ ground state, one at $\SI{461}{\nm}$ and another at $\SI{689}{\nm}$. 
The bosonic isotopes (\Sr{88}, \Sr{86}, and \Sr{84}) have no hyperfine structure since $I = 0$ and their total angular momentum is given by $\vb{J} = \vb{L} + \vb{S}$. 
The $I = {9}/{2}$ nuclear spin of \Sr{87} leads to hyperfine interactions ($\hat{V}_{\text{hf}}$) so the total angular momentum is now given by $\vb{F} = \vb{I} + \vb{J}$.
The shift due to $\hat{V}_{\text{hf}}$ can be expressed as (see e.g., \cite{Steck.QuantumAtomOptics, Woodgate.Elementary})
\begin{equation}
	\ev*{\hat{V}_{\text{hf}}}	=	\frac{1}{2} A_{\text{hf}} K + B_{\text{hf}} \frac{\frac{3}{2} K \pqty*{K + 1} - 2 I \pqty*{I + 1} J \pqty*{J + 1}}{4 I \pqty*{2 I - 1} J \pqty*{2 J - 1}}
\end{equation}
with $K = F \pqty*{F + 1} - I \pqty*{I + 1} - J \pqty*{J + 1}$.
The hyperfine constants $A_{\text{hf}}$ and $B_{\text{hf}}$ represent the strengths of the magnetic dipole hyperfine and the electric-quadrupole interactions, respectively.

\label{sec:rydberg_properties}

This section contains values which may be useful for working with Rydberg states of strontium atoms. 

\subsection{Strontium Rydberg Constant}

The value of the Rydberg constant is $R_{\infty} = \SI{109737.31568508(65)}{\per\cm}$ \cite{Mohr2016.RMP.88.035009} for an infinitely heavy nucleus.
Replacing the electron mass ($m_e$) with the reduced mass $\mu = {m_{e} M}/{\pqty*{m_{e} + M}}$ where $M$ is the mass of the nucleus gives
\begin{equation}
	R_{M}	=	\frac{\mu}{m_e} R_{\infty}	=	\frac{M}{m_{e} + M} R_{\infty}
\end{equation}
Using the electron mass $m_e = \SI{9.10938356(11)E-31}{\kg}$, $\si{\amu} = \SI{1.660539040(20)E-27}{\kg}$, and the masses of the strontium isotopes in \cref{tab:sr_phys_prop}, the mass-corrected Rydberg constants can be calculated and are given in \cref{tab:sr_Rydberg_constants}.
